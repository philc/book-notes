# For Women Only - Shaunti Feldhahn

## Gems

* Ephesians 5: husbands love wives, wives respect husbands.
  * Wives do not need to be told to love their husbands, and husbands do not need to be told to
    respect their wives, because we already easily give what we want to receive.
* While girls and women tend to have "deep, hidden questions like, Am I special? Am I lovable?",
  men and boys tend to worry "Do I measure up? Am I any good at what I do?"
* Being a provider is at the core of a man's identity; it's a big part of "being a man." It gives
  personal significance.
* Sex conveys to the husband that he is wanted and desired by his partner.
* Of all the needs that a wife meets, the one that the husband cares about the most is sex. It's a
  failure of priority if other needs (a clean house; laundry; socializing) crowd out sex. The
  husband would never prefer a cleaner house over sex.
* Male form of romance: in addition to the classic stuff of dinners out, they want to play / do guy
  things with their wife. They don't want to "have fun" exclusively with their guy friends.
  * Man: "I just want to play with you."
  * "The woman who is having fun with her husband is incredibly attractive. If you see a woman out
    playing golf with her husband, I guarantee that all the other guys are jealous."
* Women can consider an evening as romantic and complete without sex; men usually can't.

## Your love is not enough (chap 2)

* "Men would rather feel unloved than inadequate and disrespected" according to 75% of male survey
  respondents. And really, men ultimately see these as the same thing.
* In any given relationship conflict, men predominantly feel disrespected, whereas women predominantly feel
  unloved (based on survey results).
* When in conflict, women cry when feeling unloved, and men show anger when feeling disrespected.
  Both are forms of not controlling one's emotions.
* Unconditional respect: "Just as you need the man in your life to love you unconditionally, even
  when you're not particularly lovable, your man needs you to demonstrate your respect for him
  regardless of whether he's meeting your expectations at the moment."
* Ephesians 5: husbands love wives, wives respect husbands.
  * Wives do not need to be told to love their husbands, and husbands do not need to be told to
    respect their wives, because we already easily give what we want to receive.
* The Crazy Cycle: "the unfortunate dynamic where your man doesn't give enough love, so you don't
  give enough respect."
  * How to break this cycle proactively, without relying on the man to do it? The woman can choose
    to respect while still in that cycle, and that will break her side of it. Second, feelings
    follow words and actions; if the woman says encouraging things, her feelings will likely change.
* Need: respect his judgement. "Judgement" means knowledge, opinions, decisions.
  * This means showing deference and arguing less.
  * Often, men feel their judgement is valued more at work than at home.
* Need: respect his abilities
  * "Few things are as powerful to a man as feeling that he has tried something, accomplished it,
    done well -- and someone noticed."
  * While girls and women tend to have "deep, hidden questions like, Am I special? Am I lovable?",
    men and boys tend to worry "Do I measure up? Am I any good at what I do?"
* Need: respect in public
  * This is the minimum requirement for showing respect. Teasing in public is not acceptable.
  * Men will avoid disrespect in public at any cost. Conversely, women who build up their man in
    public make a huge deposit in the relationship.
    * "From the men I've talked to, [it's] the equivalent of his coming home to you with a dozen
      roses and a surprise date night without the kids. He will feel *adored*."
* Need: show respect in our assumptions
  * Asking "have you done it yet?" carries the assumption that the man needs a reminder from his
    wife to get it done.
    * Alternative framing: I asked, he acknowledged, but hasn't done it yet. I trust my husband. So
      there is a reason he hasn't done it.
* "We as women hold incredible power... and opportunity" to show respect.
* If you "always assume the best, you will find it easier to show respect."

## Performance of a lifetime (chap 3)

* A man's insecurity is that he will fail at the job, and be judged incompetent.
* "Guys can use that fear of failure for good. It gets you out of bed in the morning. It gets you to
  the gym when you wouldn't go otherwise."
* If a man is affirmed at home, he's empowered to do well in every other area of his life. It
  starts at home.
* Create a "haven" at home. The author argues that "men need a place where they can make their
  mistakes in peace," unlike at the workplace, where they are judged.
* A wife's gift of encouragement: send the man into the world full of confidence.

## The thinker (chap 4)

* When a man retreats during a conflict to be alone and process, women shouldn't interpret that as
  withdrawing or him showing that he doesn't care.
* The author asserts women tend to be verbal processors, and men tend to be internal processors.
* After an argument men need a cooling period to be able to rationally engage.
  * (Why is this conveyed as gender specific -- isn't the same generally true of women?)
* Work with the way men are wired, rather than getting upset about them being wired differently and
  trying to change it.

## The loneliest burden (chap 5)

* The man cannot depend on his wife to provide for the family's needs. His identity says "it's my
  job."
* Men think about their responsibility for providing, all the time.
  * (Don't single women think about providing for themselves all the time as well?)
* "Men feel powerful when they provide. And they want to be depended on."
* Being a provider is at the core of a man's identity; it's a big part of "being a man." It gives
  personal significance.
* Providing is one of the primary ways that men say "I love you."
* The desire to provide is also combined with an "internal drive to succeed and find pleasure in
  their work." I.e. it's not entirely selfless -- it can be enjoyable in that it satisfies their
  desire to be a successful competent person.
* "Every day, providers can feel a strange tension between wanting to be depended on and feeling
  trapped by that responsibility."
* "When a man gets a social security or pension check without feeling like he is earning it, he
  feels like he has lost his purpose." (from the survey results).
* A wife can help ease the burden by showing that she is willing to refuse to spend money
  unnecessarily.
* And "thank him regularly for providing."

## Sex changes everything (chap 6)

* Sex conveys to the husband that he is wanted and desired by his partner.
* Feeling desired provides a sense of confidence and well-being in the other areas of his life.
* "Sex is a release of day-to-day pressures... and seems to make everything else better"
  * (Sounds similar to sleep, "the background music of life.")
* Know that if you reject sex, "you're touching his *heart*." If you really have to decline due to
  tiredness or other reasons, "let your words be heart words -- reassuring, affirming, adoring."
* Of all the needs that a wife meets, the one that the husband cares about the most is sex. It's a
  failure of priority if other needs (a clean house; laundry; socializing) crowd out sex. The
  husband would never prefer a cleaner house over sex.

## Keeper of the photo files (chap 7)

* "The unique wiring of the male brain creates an instinctive pull to visually consume the image of
  an attractive woman."
  * (Why is this notable -- is this not the case for women?)
* Analogy: our brains will unavoidably notice food in the room, and then it's up to our reasoning
  center and willpower to decide what to do next -- indulge, or ignore it.
* (There's a summary of neuroscience findings about how male vs. female brains light up when seeing
  an attractive member of the opposite sex)
  * The male brains' reaction to attractive woman is physical. A woman's reaction to an attractive
    male is cognitive/thinking.
* Men have sensual images pop into their heads often -- all the time.
  * (Does this really not happen to women? The book says "it never does." I don't believe it.)
  * Women tend to have emotional memories "pop in", rather than images. Perhaps because their brains
    are equipped more for emotional processing.
* Reassurance: "Most men I spoke with said they enjoy being visual but wish they could reserve it
  entirely for their wives."
* Reassurance: "He can see a beautiful woman without the sexual temptation." It can be admiring
  beauty, as one admires a sculpture.
* God created all men this way, for some reason. Even Job had to make a covenant not to look upon
  women with lust, and even before doing so, God described him as "the finest man in all the earth."
* Anecdote: "There is no relationship with the woman who catches your eye. With your wife, you have
  a deep and long and meaningful relationship. There is no competition there."

## Chocolate, flowers, bait fishing (chap 8)

* A chapter about how men want to be romantic, and why they aren't more often.
* Most women do not appreciate "how tough it is for a man to switch from the fast-paced, highly
  practical attitude of work to the tenderness of romance."
  * For many men, there needs to be some decompression period after work. It might be taking a walk
    with his wife and debriefing with her on how his day went.
* Male form of romance: in addition to the classic stuff of dinners out, they want to play / do guy
  things with their wife. They don't want to "have fun" exclusively with their guy friends.
  * Man: "I just want to play with you."
  * "The woman who is having fun with her husband is incredibly attractive. If you see a woman out
    playing golf with her husband, I guarantee that all the other guys are jealous."
* Women can consider an evening as romantic and complete without sex; men usually can't.
* If the husband has desire and motivation for romance but is frustrated (e.g. was criticized on past
  attempts), the wife has great power to unlock more romance for herself: encourage, affirm, allow
  him to chase her.

## The truth about taking care of yourself (chap 9)

* A chapter about maintaining physical appearance / beauty; sensitive topic for most women.
* Men want her to make an effort to be attractive for him.
* This means in shape, put together, and has energy to do activities together.
* "Since men are so visual, seeing us make the effort to look good makes them feel loved and cared
  for."
* Good news: "every man I talked with said he would do whatever it takes to help his partner make
  this particular effort."
* For women, it can be suddenly motivating to do the work when they realize that it's so important
  to their husbands.

## Words for your heart (chap 10)

* #1 survey response to the question "the one thing I wish she knew": "How much I love her."
